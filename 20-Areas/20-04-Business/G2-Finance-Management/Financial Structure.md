4. Leveraging
- Stage 1: Acquisition Debt (Positive Spread with Prior Stress Testing; Positive Spread-Supporting Interest Rates; Compounding Priorisation; Asset Acceleration; Timing That Wins In All Market Cycles (Expansion (Cheap Credit, High Confidence, Rising Assets, Ease of Borrowing—> Prepare Capital Reserves), Peak (Prices Feel Unstoppable, Widespread Greed, Risk Tolerance Is Inflated—Slow Down Be Selective), Contraction (Spreading Fear, Price Softening, Credit Tightening, Quietly Emerging Opportunities—>Deploy Capital, Acquire at Discount), Recovery (Confidence Rebuilding, Returning Stability, Early Movers Benefit), 

- Stage 2: Optimisation Debt (Stable Assets, Growth In Equity, Consistent Cash Flow —> Strategic Repayments: Refinance for Better Terms (When Rates Drop Drastically, Refinance to Reduce Cost Capital and Increase Spread; When Equity Increases, Refinance to Access Growth Capital, But Only If Redeployment Produces Positive Spread), Extend Duration, Lower Interest Rate, Improve Cash Flow, or Strategically Eliminate (Due to Disappeared Spread, Interest Rate Exceeds Asset Return, Risk Outweighs Reward, Peace Outweighs Leveraging))—But Keep Debt That Has a Positive Spread

- Stage 3: Optional Debt (Assets Producing Strong Cash Flow, Substantial Equity, and Healthy Reserves—>Not Forced to Leverage)
